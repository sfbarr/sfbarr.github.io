About Me

I'm a recent Computer Science graduate from San Francisco State University (GPA 3.75) who started out studying neuroscience at NYU and became an EMT — medicine was always the plan. Somewhere along the way I discovered software engineering, and realized I could do more for patients and researchers by building the tools they need than by working at the bench.

Across 20+ projects — from physics simulators and audio visualizers to medical research dashboards and adventure RPGs — I've shipped RESTful APIs, trained hybrid CNN/RNN models, and co-authored a spinal cord injury meta-analysis for the Journal of Neurotrauma alongside researchers and clinicians at Unite 2 Fight Paralysis.

I'm actively seeking full-time roles at the intersection of software and healthcare, where a background in both CS and medicine is a feature, not a footnote.
